NVIDIA Strategy Comparison Report

This report analyzes three simple, long-only trading strategies on NVIDIA using the provided OHLCV data and adjusted prices. We compare performance across the full sample and across three non-overlapping chronological segments (early, middle, late thirds). We answer: which strategy grew a $1,000 initial investment the most and least per period; how risks differed via max drawdowns; and whether patterns are consistent. Signals are applied without forward-looking bias (positions act the next day), results use Adjusted Close, duplicate/missing dates are handled, and a simple per-trade cost is stated so results are net of costs.

Data Handling and Assumptions

Data summary: 6393 rows after cleaning; date range 1999-01-22 to 2024-06-18.

  • Duplicates by date removed: 0; rows with missing Adjusted Close dropped: 0.
  • Dates parsed and sorted chronologically; all computations are forward-looking-bias-free: positions for a day are based on signals from prior data and take effect the next trading day.
  • Price basis: Adjusted Close used for returns, signals, and equity curves to account for corporate actions.
  • Transaction costs: assumed 0.05% per position change (e.g., 0→1 or 1→0) on traded notional; all results are net of these costs.
  • Strategy parameters were chosen solely based on segment length (number of observations) before backtesting and then held fixed within each segment; no future data were used to set parameters (no look-ahead).

Strategies Evaluated

  • Buy-and-Hold baseline using Adjusted Close.
  • Moving-average trend-following: long when short MA > long MA; otherwise in cash. Full-sample windows around 20/100 (short/long); exact per-period parameters were fixed ex-ante and are listed below.
  • Time-series momentum: long when lookback return > 0, otherwise in cash. Full-sample lookback around 60 trading days; exact per-period parameters were fixed ex-ante and are listed below.
